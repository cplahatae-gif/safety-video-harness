# Semi-Realistic Industrial Safety Style Guide

## Purpose

Create safety-training keyframes that feel close to a real Korean industrial site while staying clean enough for instruction.

## Visual Direction

- Semi-realistic industrial rendering, not cartoon, not photorealistic surveillance footage.
- Use the approved ready-mix concrete plant and worker reference images as the primary visual anchor.
- Keep surfaces practical and readable: concrete yard, silos, mixer trucks, lane markings, cones, mirrors, and PPE must be easy to identify.
- Use natural daylight and restrained contrast so blind zones, pedestrian routes, and signal gestures remain visible.

## Character And PPE

- Workers wear gray-blue industrial uniforms, hard hats, safety vests when needed, and black safety shoes.
- Keep recurring worker identity, body type, uniform colors, and role consistent across frames.
- Every gaze must point toward a visible safety reason: mirror, camera display, signal person, vehicle route, hazard zone, or pedestrian path.

## Vehicle And Site

- Mixer truck and plant geometry should remain stable across adjacent keyframes.
- Preserve the same ready-mix plant yard, blue-white industrial color cues, silos, wet concrete surface, and vehicle scale.
- Hazard zones, pedestrian routes, and vehicle lanes must be visually separated and consistent.

## Safety Framing

- Show near-miss prevention, not impact.
- Do not show injury, gore, panic, or dramatic crash spectacle.
- Do not rely on generated Korean or English text inside keyframes. Text belongs in subtitle, overlay, or title-card artifacts.

## Negative Constraints

- No readable logos.
- No random extra workers.
- No sudden PPE changes.
- No disconnected checklist panels.
- No cinematic exaggeration that hides the education point.
